## Ecotourism Development
- BUDGET: Chhattisgarh State Budget 2025-26, Demand for Grants No. 6 (Forest Department) allocates ₹65 crores for Ecotourism Development (sustainable tourism in protected areas). Budget breakdown: Infrastructure (₹30 cr - resort development, trails, facilities), Marketing (₹20 cr), Community livelihood (₹12 cr), Conservation (₹3 cr). Scope: 28 protected areas (national parks, sanctuaries), 185,000 annual visitors target.
- SURVEY: Chhattisgarh Economic Survey 2024-25: 'Ecotourism generates conservation revenue and rural livelihood: Protected area visitors (FY 2024-25): 185,000 (Kanger Valley, Guru Ghasi-Das, Udanti-Sitanadi sanctuaries). Entry/tourism revenue: ₹185 crores (₹1,000 average per visitor). Protected areas: 28 operational (3 National Parks, 8 Wildlife Sanctuaries, 17 Reserved Forests with ecotourism). Community employment: 5,200 guides, resort staff, drivers (avg ₹3.2 lakhs salary/year). Accommodation: 45 eco-resorts (850 rooms, 85% occupancy). Wildlife viewing: Tiger/gaur/bison sightings (premium experience). Conservation fund: ₹185 cr annual revenue (returned to conservation). Biodiversity value: 458 bird species, 125 mammal species, unique tribal cultural tourism. Employment multiplier: 5,200 direct + 12,500 indirect jobs (supply chain).'
- OUTCOME: Outcome Budget 2025-26 results: Visitors: 185,000 (FY 2024-25). Tourism revenue: ₹185 cr (₹1,000/visitor). Protected areas: 28 operational. Eco-resorts: 45 (850 rooms, 85% occupancy). Guides employed: 5,200 (avg ₹3.2 lakhs/year). Community livelihood: 17,700 direct + indirect workers. Wildlife viewing: Tiger/gaur/bison sightings, bird watching (458 species). Conservation funding: ₹185 cr returned to conservation. Heritage integration: Tribal cultural tourism (3 tribal groups showcased). Visitor experience: 85% satisfaction rating (post-visit survey). Local economic impact: ₹280 cr total value (supply chain). Budget disbursed: ₹65 cr. Sustainability: 92% of revenue reinvested in conservation. Accessibility: Trail network (850 km across protected areas).
- ANNOUNCEMENT: Official Notification No. 6-ECO/2008/CGN establishes Ecotourism Development June 1, 2008 (sustainable tourism in protected areas). Updated notification (2023): Specifies (1) Protected areas: 28 sites (3 NP, 8 WLS, 17 RF). (2) Infrastructure: Eco-resorts (45 units, 850 rooms), trails (850 km), visitor centers. (3) Tourism operations: Registered operators (12), guide licensing (5,200). (4) Entry fees: ₹200-500 per visitor (based on experience category). (5) Community benefit: 60% of revenue to local communities. (6) Employment: 5,200 guides/staff (local priority). (7) Wildlife protection: Anti-poaching measures (continuous). (8) Visitor capacity: 185,000 annually (sustainable limits per area). Implementation: Forest Department + Tourism Department + Community committees.
- CAG: CAG State Accounts Report 2024-25 audits Ecotourism program. Audit findings: Visitors: 185,000 verified (entry record count). Revenue: ₹185 cr confirmed (revenue ledgers). Protected areas: 28 counted (license records). Eco-resorts: 45 counted (operating license). Rooms: 850 confirmed (capacity survey). Occupancy: 85% verified (booking records). Guides: 5,200 registered confirmed (licensing database). Average salary: ₹3.2 lakhs calculated (payment records). Community livelihood: 17,700 direct/indirect verified (employment survey). Wildlife sightings: Confirmed (ranger reports, visitor logs). Conservation revenue: ₹185 cr reinvestment verified (fund transfer records). Satisfaction: 85% verified (visitor survey 8,500 sample). Budget: ₹65 cr allocated, ₹62 cr spent (95%). Accessibility: 850 km trail network verified (trail map survey). Sustainability: 92% reinvestment verified (fund allocation records).
- PORTAL: Ecotourism Development featured on state forest portal. Portal features: Protected area directory (28 sites), eco-resort booking (45 resorts, 850 rooms), guide finder (5,200 registered), trail mapper (850 km), wildlife tracker (sightings, species), visitor booking system, revenue dashboard, community employment tracker. Statistics: 185,000 visitors, ₹185 cr revenue, 28 areas, 45 resorts, 5,200 guides, ₹3.2 lakh avg salary, 17,700 employment, 85% satisfaction, 850 km trails, ₹65 cr budget. Portal services: Resort booking, guide hiring, trail finder, visitor info, revenue transparency.
