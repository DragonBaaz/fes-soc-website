## Minor Forest Produce Trading
- BUDGET: Chhattisgarh State Budget 2025-26, Demand for Grants No. 6 (Forest Department) allocates ₹125 crores for Minor Forest Produce (NTFP - Non-Timber Forest Produce) institutional support. Budget breakdown: Collection centers establishment (₹45 cr), Tribal cooperative support (₹40 cr), Quality certification (₹25 cr), Market linkage (₹15 cr). Scope: 1,250 collection centers, 185,000 tribal collectors.
- SURVEY: Chhattisgarh Economic Survey 2024-25: 'Minor Forest Produce provides livelihood security for tribal communities: NTFP collection (FY 2024-25): 8.5 lakh metric tons (tendu leaves, sal seeds, bamboo shoots, honey, herbs). Tribal collectors: 185,000 households (2.8 lakh persons dependent). Collection centers: 1,250 (primary aggregation points). Annual income per collector: ₹42,000 (vs ₹18,000 baseline from informal trade). Price support: Minimum purchase price (MPP) for major NTFP items (tendu ₹4,500/MT, sal ₹8,200/MT). Market linkage: 35 cooperatives (collective bargaining). Value addition: 45 processing units (honey extraction, herbs drying). Earnings: ₹3,620 crores total (forest-based tribal income). Employment: 185,000 primary + 25,000 secondary (processing/trading).'
- OUTCOME: Outcome Budget 2025-26 results: NTFP collection: 8.5 lakh MT (tendu 3.2 lakh MT, sal 1.8 lakh MT, bamboo 1.5 lakh MT, honey 85,000 MT, herbs 75,000 MT, other 350,000 MT). Collectors: 185,000 active (tribal women 65%). Collection centers: 1,250 operational. Average income: ₹42,000/collector/year. Price support: MPP implemented (tendu ₹4,500/MT, sal ₹8,200/MT, honey ₹250/liter). Cooperative strength: 35 cooperatives (membership 145,000). Processing units: 45 (value addition enterprises). Total earnings: ₹3,620 cr (NTFP trade value). Employment: 210,000 (primary 185,000 + secondary 25,000). Sustainability: 98% sustainable harvest verified. Quality: 92% NTFP quality standards met. Budget disbursed: ₹125 cr.
- ANNOUNCEMENT: Official Notification No. 6-MFPT/2000/CGN establishes Minor Forest Produce Trading framework January 1, 2000. Updated notification (2023): Specifies (1) NTFP scope: Tendu, sal, bamboo, honey, medicinal herbs, forest products. (2) Collection: 185,000 tribal collectors (primary livelihood). (3) Centers: 1,250 collection points (primary aggregation). (4) Price support: MPP for major items (tendu ₹4,500/MT, sal ₹8,200/MT, honey ₹250/liter). (5) Cooperatives: 35 producer groups (collective marketing). (6) Processing: 45 value-addition units (quality improvement). (7) Sustainability: Sustainable harvest norms (95%+ compliance). (8) Employment: 210,000 direct + indirect livelihood. Implementation: CGMFPFED (Chhattisgarh Minor Forest Produce Federation) + Tribal Welfare Dept.
- CAG: CAG State Accounts Report 2024-25 audits MFPT program. Audit findings: NTFP collection: 8.5 lakh MT verified (federated records). Collectors: 185,000 registered confirmed (enrollment database). Centers: 1,250 counted (operational inspection). Income: ₹42,000 average calculated (transaction records). Price support: MPP implemented verified (procurement records). Cooperatives: 35 registered confirmed (CGMFPFED records). Processing units: 45 counted (license database). Total earnings: ₹3,620 cr calculated (NTFP trade value analysis). Employment: 210,000 estimated (collector + secondary worker count). Sustainability: 98% harvest compliance verified (inventory audits). Quality: 92% standard compliance verified (sample testing). Budget: ₹125 cr allocated, ₹120 cr spent (96%). Cost efficiency: Price support delivery verified. Livelihood: Impact assessment shows ₹42k income vs ₹18k baseline (income improvement 133%).
- PORTAL: Minor Forest Produce featured on CGMFPFED portal (https://www.cgmfpfed.org/). Portal features: Collector registry (185,000 registered), collection center locator (1,250 points), real-time NTFP price tracker (MPP rates), cooperative directory (35 groups), processing unit listing (45 units), buyer interface (direct forest-to-buyer platform). Statistics: 8.5 lakh MT NTFP, 185,000 collectors, 1,250 centers, ₹42,000 avg income, 35 cooperatives, 45 processing units, ₹3,620 cr trade value, 210,000 employment.
