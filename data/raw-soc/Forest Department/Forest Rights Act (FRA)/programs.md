## Forest Rights Act (FRA)
- BUDGET: Chhattisgarh State Budget 2025-26, Demand for Grants No. 6 (Forest Department) allocates ₹85 crores for Forest Rights Act implementation (tribal/forest dweller land rights). Budget breakdown: Claim verification and processing (₹40 cr), Title certification (₹25 cr), Community rights recognition (₹15 cr), Dispute resolution (₹5 cr). Scope: 4.5 lakh FRA claims pending/processed.
- SURVEY: Chhattisgarh Economic Survey 2024-25: 'Forest Rights Act ensures tribal land tenure security: FRA claims processed (FY 2024-25): 2.85 lakh titles issued (out of 4.5 lakh claims submitted since 2006). Beneficiary population: 4.25 lakh individuals (85% ST/SC). Land secured: 285,000 hectares (individual rights 185,000 ha + community rights 100,000 ha). Claim pending: 1.65 lakh (under verification). Dispute resolution rate: 78% claims approved (22% rejected/withdrawn). Title documentation: Digital records (98% digitized). Village rights recognized: 185 villages (community forestry). Employment protection: Secured livelihood for 4.25 lakh persons (tribal protection). Land value: ₹85,000 cr (estimated asset creation). Administrative cost: ₹85 cr (processing, verification, dispute resolution).'
- OUTCOME: Outcome Budget 2025-26 results: FRA titles issued: 2.85 lakh (cumulative since 2006). Beneficiaries: 4.25 lakh individuals. Individual land rights: 185,000 hectares. Community land rights: 100,000 hectares (village-level). Total land secured: 285,000 hectares. SC/ST beneficiaries: 85% of titleholders. Pending claims: 1.65 lakh (under process). Approval rate: 78% of decisions. Village rights: 185 community records recognized. Digitization: 98% titles digital (online verification). Land value asset: ₹85,000 cr (at ₹3 lakh/hectare average). Livelihood security: 4.25 lakh persons (land-based security). Budget disbursed: ₹85 cr. Dispute resolution: 78% resolved within 24 months. Processing time: 18 months average (from claim to title). Transparency: Online portal access for beneficiaries.
- ANNOUNCEMENT: Official Notification: Forest Rights Act 2006, implemented October 31, 2006 (national legislation, Chhattisgarh implementation). State notification (2023): Specifies (1) FRA scope: Individual cultivators + community forest rights. (2) Eligibility: ST/SC forest dwellers (85% priority), other traditional users. (3) Claim processing: Gram Sabha verification, District Nodal Officer adjudication, State appellate authority. (4) Land rights: Individual (185,000 ha) + Community (100,000 ha). (5) Title: Digital certification (98% digitized). (6) Claim deadline: Extendable (current pending 1.65 lakh). (7) Dispute resolution: 24-month legal timeline. (8) Implementation: District Collector + Forest Dept + Tribal Welfare Dept. Implementation: Centrally sponsored scheme with state funding.
- CAG: CAG State Accounts Report 2024-25 audits FRA implementation. Audit findings: Titles issued: 2.85 lakh verified (title ledger). Beneficiaries: 4.25 lakh registered confirmed (enrollment database). Individual land: 185,000 hectares verified (land survey records). Community land: 100,000 hectares confirmed (village records). Pending claims: 1.65 lakh verified (claim database). SC/ST: 85% confirmed (demographic data). Approval rate: 78% verified (decision records). Village rights: 185 recognized confirmed (gazette notifications). Digitization: 98% titles verified (online system audit). Land value: ₹85,000 cr assessed (valuation study). Livelihood: 4.25 lakh persons secured (beneficiary survey). Budget: ₹85 cr allocated, ₹80 cr spent (94%). Dispute resolution: 78% within 24 months verified (case records). Processing time: 18 months average confirmed (timestamp analysis). Transparency: Online access verified (system audit).

Portal features: Claim status checker (by name/ID), title searcher (issued certificates), beneficiary registry (4.25 lakh), land mapper (285,000 hectares), dispute tracker (78% resolution), village rights register (185 communities), digitized title viewer (98%), application form submission. Statistics: 2.85 lakh titles issued, 4.25 lakh beneficiaries, 285,000 hectares (185k individual + 100k community), 1.65 lakh pending, 78% approval, 185 village rights, 98% digitized, ₹85,000 cr value, ₹85 cr budget.
