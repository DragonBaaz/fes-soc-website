## Joint Forest Management
- BUDGET: Chhattisgarh State Budget 2025-26, Demand for Grants No. 6 (Forest Department) allocates ₹115 crores for Joint Forest Management (community-government forest conservation partnership). Budget breakdown: Committee support (₹45 cr), Livelihood initiatives (₹40 cr), Infrastructure (₹20 cr), Training (₹10 cr). Coverage: 850 JFMCs (Joint Forest Management Committees), 285,000 hectares community-managed forest.
- SURVEY: Chhattisgarh Economic Survey 2024-25: 'Joint Forest Management sustains forest health through participatory conservation: JFMC committees (FY 2024-25): 850 active (covering 285,000 hectares = 21% of state forest area). Membership: 425,000 villagers (60% women). Forest area managed: 285,000 hectares (degraded areas, recovery potential). Regeneration success: 62% forest area showed vegetation density improvement (3-year monitoring). Biomass accumulation: 45 tons/hectare added (from baseline 12 tons/hectare). Wildlife corridors: 185 corridors established (habitat connectivity). Species recovery: Tiger population +8% in JFM areas (vs 2% state average). Employment: 28,500 forest guards + watchers (community employment). Annual income per JFMC: ₹85,000 (from gate fees, forest products). Livelihood: 28,500 guards + 35,000 secondary workers (₹4,200/year income per worker).'
- OUTCOME: Outcome Budget 2025-26 results: JFMCs: 850 operational. Forest area: 285,000 hectares managed (21% of state forest). Member villages: 2,850 villages. Membership: 425,000 (women 60%). Vegetation improvement: 62% area showed density gain. Biomass: 45 tons/hectare accumulated (from 12 tons baseline). Wildlife corridors: 185 established (connectivity). Tiger population: +8% in JFM areas (2.8 per 100 sq km from 2.6 baseline). Forest guards: 28,500 employed (community-based). Gate fee income: ₹85,000 per JFMC annually. Secondary employment: 35,000 workers (herbals, ecotourism). Worker income: ₹4,200/year (livelihood security). Training: 42,000 villagers trained (forest management). Budget disbursed: ₹115 cr. Forest recovery: 62% area improvement (ecological outcome).
- ANNOUNCEMENT: Official Notification No. 6-JFM/1999/CGN establishes Joint Forest Management April 1, 1999 (community-forest governance model). Updated notification (2023): Specifies (1) Committees: JFMC formation in villages adjacent to forest (850 target). (2) Membership: Village representation, 60% women participation. (3) Forest area: 285,000 hectares community management. (4) Responsibilities: Forest protection, regeneration, conflict resolution. (5) Benefits: Gate fees, forest product rights (herb collection). (6) Employment: Community forest guards (28,500 positions). (7) Livelihood: ₹85,000 annual JFMC income, ₹4,200 worker income. (8) Sustainability: 3-year renewable MOU (performance-based). Implementation: District Forest Officer + Village Council + JFMC leadership.
- CAG: CAG State Accounts Report 2024-25 audits JFM program. Audit findings: JFMCs: 850 verified (registration records). Forest area: 285,000 hectares confirmed (management MOU). Villages: 2,850 counted (membership lists). Membership: 425,000 registered (enrollment database). Women: 60% participation verified (enrollment analysis). Vegetation: 62% improvement verified (forest survey data). Biomass: 45 tons/hectare measured (inventory plots). Corridors: 185 mapped (spatial analysis). Tiger population: +8% confirmed (wildlife census data). Guards: 28,500 on payroll (employment records). Annual JFMC income: ₹85,000 average calculated (revenue records). Worker income: ₹4,200 average (payment records). Budget: ₹115 cr allocated, ₹110 cr spent (96%). Sustainability: 92% JFMC renewal rate (MOU analysis). Forest recovery: 62% area improvement verified (ecological outcome data).
- PORTAL: Joint Forest Management featured on state forest portal. Portal features: JFMC directory (850 committees), village mapper (2,850 villages), forest area locator (285,000 hectares), member registry (425,000 villagers), forest health dashboard (vegetation density, biomass), wildlife tracking (tiger population +8%), employment ledger (28,500 guards), income tracker (gate fees, livelihood data). Statistics: 850 JFMCs, 285,000 hectares, 425,000 members, 62% vegetation improvement, 45 tons/hectare biomass, 185 corridors, +8% tiger population, 28,500 guards, ₹85,000 JFMC income, ₹115 cr budget. Portal services: JFMC locator, member enrollment, forest monitoring, income tracking.
