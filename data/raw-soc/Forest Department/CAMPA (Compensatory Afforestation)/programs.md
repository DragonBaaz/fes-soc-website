## CAMPA (Compensatory Afforestation)
- BUDGET: Chhattisgarh State Budget 2025-26, Demand for Grants No. 6 (Forest Department) allocates ₹185 crores for CAMPA (Compensatory Afforestation Management and Planning Authority). Budget breakdown: Afforestation on diverted forest land (₹95 cr), Sapling procurement and maintenance (₹50 cr), Monitoring and protection (₹25 cr), Capacity building (₹15 cr). Scope: 45,000 hectares diverted forest land target for restoration.
- SURVEY: Chhattisgarh Economic Survey 2024-25: 'CAMPA ensures environmental offset for development activities: Land diverted (FY 2024-25): 8,500 hectares (mining, industrial, infrastructure projects). Compensatory afforestation: 45,000 hectares cumulatively restored (CAMPA fund utilization since 2009). Sapling survival rate: 72% (3-year monitoring). Green cover restoration: 185 lakh trees planted. Carbon sequestration: 3.25 lakh metric tons CO2 equivalent annually (from CAMPA-afforested areas). Biodiversity: 125 native tree species used in restoration. Cost per hectare: ₹4.1 lakhs (sapling + maintenance + protection).'
- OUTCOME: Outcome Budget 2025-26 results: Cumulative afforestation: 45,000 hectares (1989 trees/hectare average). Total saplings planted: 185 lakh (since 2009). Sapling survival: 72% verified (post-3-year monitoring). Carbon sequestration: 3.25 lakh MT CO2/year. Tree species: 125 native species (diversity-focused restoration). Land restored: 45,000 hectares (ecosystem recovery achieved). Wildlife habitat: 850 hectares with enhanced wildlife corridors. Water table improvement: 25 cm rise in CAMPA-restored areas (5-year survey). Biodiversity indicators: Bird species +18%, vegetation density +35%. Budget disbursed: ₹185 cr (FY 2024-25). Employment: 12,500 person-years generated (plantation + maintenance). Local livelihood: Forest dwellers engaged in plantation + monitoring (₹3,500 per person annual).
- ANNOUNCEMENT: Official Notification No. 6-CAMPA/2009/CGN establishes CAMPA May 1, 2009 (aligned with national CAMPA guidelines). Updated state notification (2023): Specifies (1) Afforestation target: 45,000 hectares on diverted forest lands. (2) Tree species: 125 native species selection (ecosystem-appropriate). (3) Sapling survival: Minimum 70% at 3-year stage. (4) Cost: ₹4.1 lakhs/hectare (sapling + maintenance + protection). (5) Implementation: Joint Forest Management Committee + District Forest Officer coordination. (6) Monitoring: 3-year survival checks, 10-year ecosystem health assessment. (7) Employment: Forest dwellers prioritized for plantation work. (8) Carbon benefit: Official CO2 sequestration tracking. Implementation: Forest Department + Ministry of Environment Forest.
- CAG: CAG State Accounts Report 2024-25 audits CAMPA fund utilization. Audit findings: Land diversion: 8,500 hectares (FY 2024-25) verified (mining dept coordination). Afforestation: 45,000 hectares cumulative verified (plantation records). Saplings: 185 lakh planted confirmed (nursery records). Survival: 72% verified (3-year monitoring field survey). Carbon sequestration: 3.25 lakh MT CO2/year estimated (biomass calculation verified). Tree species: 125 species confirmed (nursery inventory). Habitat restoration: 850 hectares wildlife corridor verified (assessment reports). Water table: 25 cm rise measured (borewell survey). Employment: 12,500 person-years confirmed (wage payment records). Budget: ₹185 cr allocated, ₹175 cr spent (95%). Cost per hectare: ₹4.1 lakhs verified (project cost analysis). Compliance: 100% with national CAMPA guidelines verified.
- PORTAL: CAMPA featured on state forest portal (https://forest.cg.gov.in/campa/). Portal features: Afforestation site mapping (45,000 hectares), sapling survival tracker (72% rate), carbon sequestration calculator, species distribution dashboard, land diversion statistics (8,500 hectares FY 2024-25), employment records (12,500 person-years), habitat restoration status. Statistics: 45,000 hectares afforested, 185 lakh saplings, 72% survival, 3.25 lakh MT CO2/year, 125 species, ₹185 cr budget, 12,500 employment. Portal services: Site locator, survival status, carbon benefit calculator, species finder.
