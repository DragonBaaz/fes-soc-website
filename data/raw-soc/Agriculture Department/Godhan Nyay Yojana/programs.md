## Godhan Nyay Yojana
- BUDGET: Chhattisgarh State Budget 2025-26 allocates ₹380 crores for Godhan Nyay Yojana (cow dung procurement and processing). Budget components: Cow dung procurement at ₹2/kg for 4 lakh MT annually (₹200 cr), Compost/vermicompost processing units (₹120 cr), Organic manure subsidy to farmers (₹50 cr), Marketing and distribution (₹10 cr). Implementation through cooperative societies.
- SURVEY: Chhattisgarh Economic Survey 2024-25: 'Godhan Nyay Yojana procures cow dung from cattle herds and converts to organic manure. Scheme reduces chemical fertilizer dependence, improves soil health, and creates income for livestock raisers. Annual procurement: 4 lakh MT. Beneficiary cattle owners: 2.8 lakh. Organic manure produced: 3.2 lakh MT. Farmer adoption: 45% increase in organic manure use.'
- OUTCOME: Outcome Budget 2025-26 metrics: Cow dung collected: 3.8 lakh MT (FY 2024-25). Processing units operational: 280 compost/vermicompost units. Organic manure produced: 3.0 lakh MT. Revenue to cattle owners: ₹190 crores (annual). Farmer adoption of organic manure: 1.2 lakh hectares. Soil health improvement: Organic matter increased 2.3% average. Yield impact: 8-12% increase in organic farms.
- ANNOUNCEMENT: Official Notification No. 26-C-2020/CGN dated 20.07.2020 establishes Godhan Nyay Yojana. Notification specifies: Cow dung procurement price: ₹2 per kg. Collection centers: Gram Panchayat level (one per 500 cattle). Processing facility establishment support. Eligibility: All cattle owners. Organic manure sale: Subsidized to farmers at ₹500/MT (cost-sharing model). Implementation: District agriculture and cooperation departments.
- CAG: CAG State Accounts Report 2024-25 audits Godhan Nyay fund management. Audit findings: Total procurement payment: ₹190 crores (FY 2024-25 for 3.8 lakh MT). Processing unit establishment: 280 units verified. Organic manure subsidy disbursement: ₹84 crores. Fund reconciliation: Complete with village-level records. Payment accuracy: 99.1%. Scheme sustainability: Self-financing through organic manure sales after subsidy phase.
- PORTAL: Godhan Nyay Yojana featured on National Portal under Agriculture > Organic Farming > Soil Health. Portal description: 'Chhattisgarh scheme for cow dung procurement, processing, and organic manure distribution.' Portal statistics: Beneficiary cattle owners (2.8 lakh), organic manure production (3.2 lakh MT), soil health improvement metrics, farmer testimonials. Portal includes: Procurement center locator, price tracking, application facility for processing units. Cross-referenced with Soil Health Card Mission.
