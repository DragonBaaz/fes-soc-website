## Artificial Insemination Program
- BUDGET: Budget allocation for genetic improvement through artificial insemination (AI). Annual budget includes: semen collection and preservation technology, AI technician training, equipment procurement, genetic material storage facilities, farmer awareness programs. Allocated under livestock genetic improvement.
- SURVEY: Economic Survey: 'Artificial Insemination Program improves livestock genetic quality without crossbreeding. Program enables farmers to upgrade animal stock through high-genetic value semen. Technology adoption increases milk yield and meat quality, improving farmer income.'
- OUTCOME: Outcome metrics: AI procedures completed annually. Genetic improvement rate documented. Calves born from high-quality semen. Herd productivity improvement measured. Farmer satisfaction and adoption rates tracked. Milk yield increase post-AI recorded.
- ANNOUNCEMENT: Official notification for AI program implementation. Notification outlines: AI service provider network, farmer eligibility and registration, semen quality standards, operation procedure (SOP), cost-sharing mechanism, and performance expectations for genetic improvement.
- CAG: CAG State Accounts 2024-25 examines AI program fund utilization. Audit verifies: Semen preservation facility maintenance cost, AI technician salary disbursement, training program expenses, equipment procurement and maintenance, and genetic improvement success rates.
- PORTAL: Artificial Insemination program featured on National Portal under Agriculture > Animal Husbandry > Genetic Improvement. Portal details service availability, farmer registration, semen quality certification, technician network, and genetic gain documentation. Linked with national livestock improvement mission.
